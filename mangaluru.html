Must Places to Visit:


01. Panambur Beach

  • Panambur Beach is one of the safest and best maintaines beach of India.
  • It is situated 10km Noth of Mangalore City Center.
  • Important Events: Beach Festival, Kite Festival
  • Other attractions include jet ski rides, boating, dolphin viewing, food stalls and is also known for its highly skilled and trained lifeguards who patrol the beach to ensure visitor safety.
  • This beach has been popular for its sunsets, the port area and a picnic spot for tourists and locals alike and offers views of the sunset. The beach attracts visitors due to its close proximity to the city.
  • Timings to visit Panambur Beach is from 8am to 7pm.


02. Manglore Beach

  • Timings: It is open 24/7.
  • Entry Charges: You don’t have to pay any entry charges.


03. Ullal Beach

  • location: 10km south from the city of Manglore.
  • Attractions are its picturesque stretch of Coconut trees, fishermen's lane, the ruined fort of Abbakka Devi and 16th century Jain temples. A resort is also situated nearby.
  • Timings: You can visit Ullal Beach anytime, but evening hours would be the best.
  • Entry Charges: There are no entry charges.


04. New manglore Port

  • Timings: Open always.
  • We can see many of the import and export operations in the port, which includes granite, iron ore, cashew, coffee, and spices. You must list the port in your itinerary.
  • Entry Charges: You have to pay an entry fee of Rs. 200/- per person.


05. Tannirbhavi Beach

  • This is the perfect place for the peace and nature lover.
  • Tannirbhavi is the quietest and offbeat location that people generally fail to visit. The place has all remained desolate and has always been kept apart from other places, just because of the isolated region.
  • The beach offers you an unruffled state, just enjoying the sunset in the evening. The only route to visit the beach is by road; it is quite close to Gurupura River or from the Sultan Battery.
  • Timings: Monday to Sunday, from 6 am to 10 pm.
  • Entry Charges: The entry is absolutely free.


06. Sasihithu Beach

  • You have an excellent opportunity to be in a serene mode spending a whole day in the Sasihithlu beach. Sasihithlu is the most visited location in the state.
  • So, you can dedicate a day of your trip just to visit the beaches in the city. The sunset is a view that we can even die for.
  • Timings: You can visit the beach between 8 am and 7.30pm.
  • Entry Charges: Free of cost entry.


07. Kudroli Gokarnath Temple

  • Deity: Gokarnanatha
  • The temple is 2 km from the centre of Mangalore city.
  • Timings: To visit the temple you have to schedule your visiting hours between 6 am and 7 pm.
  • Entry Charges: There are no entry charges.


08. Kadri Manjunath Temple

  • The architecture is solely a blend of both Indian and Buddhist culture.
  • Timings: Monday to Sunday, between 6 am to 1 pm and 4 pm to 8 pm.
  • Entry Charges: No entry fee.


09. Light-House Hill Garden

  • The garden is situated in the center of the Mangalore city drawing the attention of plenty of visitors and tourists from around the corners of the globe.
  • You can enjoy the sea view from the top of the light-house, which makes it more beautiful during the sunset.
  • The cool breeze, the sounds of soft waves, the chirping of birds and people all around gives a great calmness to mind, body, and soul.
  • Timings: You can visit the garden from 9 am to 12 pm & 4 pm to 9 pm.
  • Entry Charges: There is no entry fee.
